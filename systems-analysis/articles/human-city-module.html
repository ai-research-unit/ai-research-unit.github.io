Human City — Specialisation Module

Reads with the Human Framework, never alone. Adds specialised aspects to each dimension.

D01. Physical Dimension

Site & Setting: The terrain, waterfront, climate, and hazards of the ground the city sits on, and the regional hinterland the dense core draws food, water, and energy from — the founding site and what it imposes.

Built Fabric & Morphology: The physical city itself — density, street grid, building stock, vertical and horizontal form — the made stage daily life runs through.

Infrastructure Networks: The trunk systems as standing built stock — water, sewer, power, transit, roads — their capacity, condition, and reach across the fabric.

Saturation & Decay: The load density concentrates and the surroundings have not cleared — air and water pollution, accumulated waste, heat — and the decaying fabric: deferred repair, dereliction, ruin; cleared or left to accrete.

D02. Biological Dimension

Population Body & Density: The inhabitants as bodies — headcount, residential density, age and health profile — the living mass the city concentrates, read at the grain a city is analysed.

Sanitation & Disease Load: The endemic and epidemic disease profile crowding and water and sanitation produce, plus traffic and environmental mortality — the biological cost of density.

Care Capacity: The hospitals, clinics, and emergency response the city holds and their reach across districts, and whether capacity meets the load density generates.

Migration-Driven Renewal: How the population is sustained and grown — natural increase against the net inflow from hinterland and abroad that cities lean on — and the turnover that flow imposes.

Decline & Dependency: The standing load the life-cycle leaves concentrated here — the aged, infirm, and indigent — and the mortality density imposes; carried or left to accrete.

D03. Economic Dimension

Economic Base & Function: What the city produces and exports to the wider system — its specialisation and role in the regional and national division of labour.

Land, Property & Rent: The land market as the city's dominant asset and rent as its dominant cost — prices, ownership, and the access or exclusion the rent gradient produces.

Local Economy & Employment: The service and consumption economy the base supports and the jobs and livelihoods it sustains or fails to.

Municipal Finance: The city's own fiscal base — what it raises and what it depends on from the polity above — and where the spending goes; sub-sovereign, its room to act is set above it.

Vacancy & Misallocation: The financial residue density leaves — stranded and vacant property, malinvested development, fiscal shortfall — and the material discard the city routes to its sinks; cleared or left to accrete.

D04. Social Dimension

Spatial Stratification: How class, ethnicity, and status sort across districts — the segregation and mixing the city's geography produces, and whether the lines are hardening or dissolving.

Municipal Apparatus & Governance: The city government, agencies, policing, and services the city is run through — their competence and reach — and the nested position under regional and national authority that sets much of what it may decide.

Neighbourhood & Association: The community fabric below the whole — districts, associations, the informal networks and mutual aid that organise daily life block by block, and whether it is thickening or thinning.

Daily Flux & the Transient City: The commuter inflow and visitor presence that swell the day population above the resident base — the city used without being inhabited — and the strain that churn places on the fabric.

Disorder & the Excluded: The friction the fabric throws off and the order the apparatus fails to hold — crime, the segregated outcast, the unhoused — cleared or left to accrete.

D05. Emotional Dimension

Civic Will & Leadership: The drive to govern and direct the city — the leadership and machine that concentrate it, the appetite to build, reform, or coast — read off what the city pursues, not what it proclaims.

Public Mood & Trust: The ambient temper of the populace — confidence or grievance, pride or siege — and the radius of trust among strangers a dense city does or does not sustain, and which way it is moving.

Civic Disaffection: The estrangement, apathy, and spent will the populace carries — the alienation density and inequality leave behind — re-anchored or left to settle, feeding the disorder in Social.

D06. Informational Dimension

Civic Channels & Media: How word moves through the city — local press, signage, wayfinding, addressing and notification systems — the reach and speed of the public information layer across districts.

Languages & Codes: The tongues and scripts a dense, often polyglot population holds — the shared communicative ground and who is excluded from it by language or literacy.

Surveillance & Records: The monitoring a dense city accumulates — cameras, sensors, transit and identity data — and the municipal registers that fix who and what is where, and what the record captures or misses.

Noise & Stale Record: The rumour, misinformation, and decayed or contradictory municipal records the channels throw off — cleared or left to accrete.

D07. Moral Dimension

Inherited Law & Municipal Ordinance: The codified order the city runs on — the national and regional law it inherits, plus its own bylaws, zoning, and ordinances — and what the municipal layer adds or constrains atop the inherited floor.

Enforcement & Adjudication: How the code is actually applied — policing, courts, inspection, the evenness of enforcement across districts and classes — practice read against the rule.

Impunity & the Unredressed: The gap between justice owed and delivered — uneven enforcement, the protected, the unpunished and the over-punished — feeding the Social grievance stock; cleared or left to accrete.

D08. Intellectual Dimension

Knowledge Institutions & Talent Pool: The universities, research bodies, and skilled-labour concentration the city holds — the stock of expertise agglomeration draws and produces — and how widely competence spreads across the population.

Schooling & Skill Formation: The education system that forms the population — its quality, reach, and unevenness across districts — and the trades and competencies the local economy reproduces.

Innovation Frontier: The active frontier of method and craft the talent density works — what is being made and improved here.

Brain Drain & Obsolete Craft: The skilled outflow that hollows the talent pool and the trades the economy no longer needs — knowledge decayed or departed, cleared or left to accrete.

D09. Cultural Dimension

Civic Identity & Origin: The story the city tells about itself — its founding, its self-image, the character it claims — the bedrock identity beneath the surface mood.

Cultural Production & Scene: The arts, music, cuisine, and distinctive urban culture the city makes and hosts — how it externalises its inner life — and the venues and districts that carry it.

Built Character & Landmark: The architectural idiom and the landmarks, monuments, and signature spaces that fix the city's visual identity.

Heritage Hollowed: The heritage and symbols worn into cliché or kitsch — the commodified quarter, the stale civic brand, the anomie where shared character has eroded — renewed or left as inert residue.

D10. Spiritual Dimension

Religious Presence & Plurality: The faiths the city concentrates and their institutions, sacred sites, and reach — the plurality density gathers and the relations among them.

Cosmology & Sacred Practice: The outlooks the populace holds and the ritual, observance, and sacred space through which they are enacted in the urban setting — festival, pilgrimage, prayer, or their secular absence.

Disenchantment & the Cold Sacred: The residue where belief has lapsed — the desacralised site, the repurposed church, the void density leaves where the sacred no longer answers — re-anchored or left to settle.
