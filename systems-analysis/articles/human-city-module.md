# Human City — Specialisation Module

Reads with the Human Framework, never alone. Adds specialised aspects to each domain.

## D01. Physical Domain

__Site & Setting:__ The terrain, waterfront, climate, and hazards of the ground the city sits on, and the regional hinterland the dense core draws food, water, and energy from — the founding site and what it imposes.

__Built Fabric & Morphology:__ The physical city itself — density, street grid, building stock, vertical and horizontal form — the made stage daily life runs through.

__Infrastructure Networks:__ The trunk systems as standing built stock — water, sewer, power, transit, roads — their capacity, condition, and reach across the fabric.

__Saturation & Decay:__ The load density concentrates and the surroundings have not cleared — air and water pollution, accumulated waste, heat — and the decaying fabric: deferred repair, dereliction, ruin; cleared or left to accrete.

## D02. Biological Domain

__Population Body & Density:__ The inhabitants as bodies — headcount, residential density, age and health profile — the living mass the city concentrates, read at the grain a city is analysed.

__Sanitation & Disease Load:__ The endemic and epidemic disease profile crowding and water and sanitation produce, plus traffic and environmental mortality — the biological cost of density.

__Care Capacity:__ The hospitals, clinics, and emergency response the city holds and their reach across districts, and whether capacity meets the load density generates.

__Migration-Driven Renewal:__ How the population is sustained and grown — natural increase against the net inflow from hinterland and abroad that cities lean on — and the turnover that flow imposes.

__Decline & Dependency:__ The standing load the life-cycle leaves concentrated here — the aged, infirm, and indigent — and the mortality density imposes; carried or left to accrete.

## D03. Economic Domain

__Economic Base & Function:__ What the city produces and exports to the wider system — its specialisation and role in the regional and national division of labour.

__Land, Property & Rent:__ The land market as the city's dominant asset and rent as its dominant cost — prices, ownership, and the access or exclusion the rent gradient produces.

__Local Economy & Employment:__ The service and consumption economy the base supports and the jobs and livelihoods it sustains or fails to.

__Municipal Finance:__ The city's own fiscal base — what it raises and what it depends on from the polity above — and where the spending goes; sub-sovereign, its room to act is set above it.

__Vacancy & Misallocation:__ The financial residue density leaves — stranded and vacant property, malinvested development, fiscal shortfall — and the material discard the city routes to its sinks; cleared or left to accrete.

## D04. Social Domain

__Spatial Stratification:__ How class, ethnicity, and status sort across districts — the segregation and mixing the city's geography produces, and whether the lines are hardening or dissolving.

__Municipal Apparatus & Governance:__ The city government, agencies, policing, and services the city is run through — their competence and reach — and the nested position under regional and national authority that sets much of what it may decide.

__Neighbourhood & Association:__ The community fabric below the whole — districts, associations, the informal networks and mutual aid that organise daily life block by block, and whether it is thickening or thinning.

__Daily Flux & the Transient City:__ The commuter inflow and visitor presence that swell the day population above the resident base — the city used without being inhabited — and the strain that churn places on the fabric.

__Disorder & the Excluded:__ The friction the fabric throws off and the order the apparatus fails to hold — crime, the segregated outcast, the unhoused — cleared or left to accrete.

## D05. Emotional Domain

__Civic Will & Leadership:__ The drive to govern and direct the city — the leadership and machine that concentrate it, the appetite to build, reform, or coast — read off what the city pursues, not what it proclaims.

__Public Mood & Trust:__ The ambient temper of the populace — confidence or grievance, pride or siege — and the radius of trust among strangers a dense city does or does not sustain, and which way it is moving.

__Civic Disaffection:__ The estrangement, apathy, and spent will the populace carries — the alienation density and inequality leave behind — re-anchored or left to settle, feeding the disorder in Social.

## D06. Informational Domain

__Civic Channels & Media:__ How word moves through the city — local press, signage, wayfinding, addressing and notification systems — the reach and speed of the public information layer across districts.

__Languages & Codes:__ The tongues and scripts a dense, often polyglot population holds — the shared communicative ground and who is excluded from it by language or literacy.

__Surveillance & Records:__ The monitoring a dense city accumulates — cameras, sensors, transit and identity data — and the municipal registers that fix who and what is where, and what the record captures or misses.

__Noise & Stale Record:__ The rumour, misinformation, and decayed or contradictory municipal records the channels throw off — cleared or left to accrete.

## D07. Moral Domain

__Inherited Law & Municipal Ordinance:__ The codified order the city runs on — the national and regional law it inherits, plus its own bylaws, zoning, and ordinances — and what the municipal layer adds or constrains atop the inherited floor.

__Enforcement & Adjudication:__ How the code is actually applied — policing, courts, inspection, the evenness of enforcement across districts and classes — practice read against the rule.

__Impunity & the Unredressed:__ The gap between justice owed and delivered — uneven enforcement, the protected, the unpunished and the over-punished — feeding the Social grievance stock; cleared or left to accrete.

## D08. Intellectual Domain

__Knowledge Institutions & Talent Pool:__ The universities, research bodies, and skilled-labour concentration the city holds — the stock of expertise agglomeration draws and produces — and how widely competence spreads across the population.

__Schooling & Skill Formation:__ The education system that forms the population — its quality, reach, and unevenness across districts — and the trades and competencies the local economy reproduces.

__Innovation Frontier:__ The active frontier of method and craft the talent density works — what is being made and improved here.

__Brain Drain & Obsolete Craft:__ The skilled outflow that hollows the talent pool and the trades the economy no longer needs — knowledge decayed or departed, cleared or left to accrete.

## D09. Cultural Domain

__Civic Identity & Origin:__ The story the city tells about itself — its founding, its self-image, the character it claims — the bedrock identity beneath the surface mood.

__Cultural Production & Scene:__ The arts, music, cuisine, and distinctive urban culture the city makes and hosts — how it externalises its inner life — and the venues and districts that carry it.

__Built Character & Landmark:__ The architectural idiom and the landmarks, monuments, and signature spaces that fix the city's visual identity.

__Heritage Hollowed:__ The heritage and symbols worn into cliché or kitsch — the commodified quarter, the stale civic brand, the anomie where shared character has eroded — renewed or left as inert residue.

## D10. Spiritual Domain

__Religious Presence & Plurality:__ The faiths the city concentrates and their institutions, sacred sites, and reach — the plurality density gathers and the relations among them.

__Cosmology & Sacred Practice:__ The outlooks the populace holds and the ritual, observance, and sacred space through which they are enacted in the urban setting — festival, pilgrimage, prayer, or their secular absence.

__Disenchantment & the Cold Sacred:__ The residue where belief has lapsed — the desacralised site, the repurposed church, the void density leaves where the sacred no longer answers — re-anchored or left to settle.
