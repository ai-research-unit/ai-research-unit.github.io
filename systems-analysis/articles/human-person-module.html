Human Person — Specialisation Module

Reads with the Human Framework, never alone. Adds specialised aspects to each domain.

D01. Physical Domain

Place & Climate: Where the life is set — region, settlement, the weather lived under, nature within reach — the standing stage and what it imposes on the days.

Dwelling & Daily Ground: The home and the spaces the days actually run through — their condition, light, and room, and the routes between them.

Wear & Clutter: The residue — the deferred repairs, the accumulating objects, the disorder or upkeep of the material world; cleared, carried, or closing in; possibly an informative null.

D02. Biological Domain

Body & Vitality: Age, constitution, energy, sleep, and fitness — the body's standing capacity against the days it must carry, and which way it is running.

Health Load: The conditions carried — as disclosed or formally recorded, never inferred — and their demands of treatment, regimen, and attention.

Appetites & Dependencies: The body's wants and habituations — food, drink, substances, the cravings that organize routine.

Decline & Care Needs: The residue — the body's accruing wear and the dependency it creates or soon will: the care already required and from whom; possibly an informative null in the young and well.

D03. Economic Domain

Livelihood & Income: How the life is funded — work, trade, support, rents — the streams, their reliability, and what the labor itself costs in hours and toll.

Holdings & Obligations: What is owned against what is owed — savings, assets, and possessions as worth, set against debts, dependents, and standing commitments.

Runway & Slack: The reserve between income and need — how long the life holds if its main stream stops, the cushion or its absence said plainly.

Debt Drag & Money Leaks: The residue — the accumulated mispricing of a life: compounding debts, sunk commitments still billing, the quiet leaks consuming the margin; possibly an informative null.

D04. Social Domain

Inner Circle & Bonds: The few who matter — partner, family, close friends — each bond's strength, reciprocity, and demands.

Wider Web & Standing: Acquaintance, community, peers — the breadth of the web, the standing held within it, and the belonging or solitude it adds up to.

Position, Authority & Affiliation: The person's place in the structures of power — the roles that grant authority over others and the hierarchies they answer to, at work and beyond; the parties, unions, and institutions they are aligned with and the patrons who shelter them; and how far they can move the structures above — the vote, the voice, the access, the door that opens or does not.

Obligations & Care Given: Whom the life carries — children, parents, friends in need — the claims on time and heart.

Estrangements & Lost Standing: The residue — the feuds, the drifted friendships, the circles exited or exiled from, and the authority lapsed or stripped and the disputes in their own small domain that will not resolve; mended, carried, or left to stand; possibly an informative null.

D05. Emotional Domain

Drive & Will: The felt drive that moves the person — what they want and how hard they pursue it, ambition or its absence, the resolve to act against the pull to drift; read enacted off what they actually chase, the motive beneath it carried with its basis.

Standing Affects & Attachments: The dominant currents of the inner life — the loves, fears, angers, griefs, and longings that color it, and the felt charge of the bonds; each interior claim marked attested, enacted, or imputed.

Temperament: The characteristic mood and manner — the humor, the gravity, the default emotional weather — as it shows in conduct.

Spent Will & Despair: The residue — the failure of will and the felt exhaustion the inner life accrues: burnout, apathy, the grief unmetabolized, the trust curdled to suspicion; cleared, carried, or left to accrete; possibly an informative null.

D06. Informational Domain

Tongues & Channels: The languages held and the channels lived through — what the person can say and to whom, and where their daily attention actually goes.

Record & Presence: What exists about them — the searchable trail, the file, the reputation in circulation — and how it matches the person.

Privacy Posture: What is shown and what is withheld — the managed face, the guarded zones, who is allowed to know what.

Trailing Exhaust: The residue — the old posts, rumors, records, and misattributions that follow the person; discounted, corrected, or compounding; possibly an informative null.

D07. Moral Domain

Status & Papers: The formal standing — citizenship, residence, licenses, certifications — the permissions the life runs on and the renewal load of keeping them.

Binding Contracts & Duties: The agreements that bind — employment terms, leases, custody, support orders — the codified obligations beneath the informal ones.

Record & Exposure: The formal record carried — convictions, judgments, live disputes — and the standing exposures: what could yet be brought against the life.

Unsettled Matters: The residue — the case unresolved, the judgment unpaid, the mark that follows the transgressor; possibly an informative null.

D08. Intellectual Domain

Skills & Craft: What the person can actually do — the trades and competencies held, at what demonstrated level.

Knowledge & Formation: What they know and how they came to know it — schooling, formation, the fields commanded and the gaps beside them.

Learning Habit & Curiosity: Whether and how the mind still takes in the new — the active frontier, or its quiet absence.

Obsolete Craft & Faded Knowledge: The residue — the skill the world stopped buying, the language rusting unused, the expertise decaying untaught; possibly an informative null.

D09. Cultural Domain

Self-Story & Identity: Who the person takes themselves to be — the narrative carried, the formative chapters as they tell them, the names and labels claimed.

Tastes & Expression: The aesthetic of the life — style, dress, the made and chosen things, the arts practiced or loved — how the inner is worn outward.

Outgrown Persona: The residue — the roles still worn past their truth, the self-story gone hollow where the life has moved on; possibly an informative null.

D10. Spiritual Domain

Meaning Frame: What the life is for, as held — faith, philosophy, or working creed — and the posture toward death, fate, and the larger order.

Practice & The Sacred: What is actually done — prayer, ritual, observance, the places and seasons kept, or their deliberate absence.

Lapsed Faith & The Void: The residue — where belief cooled and what fills the space it left: the unanswered nights, the substitute devotions; possibly an informative null.
