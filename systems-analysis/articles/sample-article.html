Systems Analysis Model

Summary: A ten-domain partition of any bounded human subject in a state — society, place, institution, or movement — each domain closing on a residue it discharges. The binding constraint is orthogonality: every fact maps to exactly one domain.

P01. Physical Environment

Terrain: The inert material stage — location, climate, hazard exposure, and the standing endowment of substances and energy the subject can draw on.

Residue: The sink of what the environment absorbs and the wear it leaves — waste load, pollution, weathering, ruin.

P02. Biological Environment

Substrate: Bodies, disease and mortality baseline, vitality and capacity to heal, reproduction and density against the carrying ceiling.

Residue: The standing load of age, infirmity, and death the life-cycle leaves, and how it is met.
