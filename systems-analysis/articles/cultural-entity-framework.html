Explanations

M01. What a Cultural Entity Is

A Cultural Entity is any collective identity — real or fictional — read for how it looks, mapped across ten orthogonal domains but weighted heavily toward the Cultural domain through an aesthetic, image-generating lens. The unit is one collective identity (a culture, subculture, civilization, mythic society, fictional realm, aesthetic or ideological movement, or a species with cultural traits); the question put to every domain is how it looks and feels rendered, never how it operates from within. A single through-line Summary opens the map. The four modes share the domain set and differ only in window and change-reading.

Static Portrait

The entity's standing aesthetic at a fixed phase.

Discrete Shift

A rapid stylistic rupture — conquest, conversion, revolution, contact — bracketed from break to settling.

Trajectory

A multi-generational stylistic drift with no single flashpoint, bracketed across the arc.

Speculative Construction

A coherent invented identity extrapolated from established material and aesthetic limits.

M02. Core Design Principles

Six invariants govern the map — orthogonality, aliveness, metabolic closure, significance ordering, scale invariance, evidence discipline. This projection fixes them to an aesthetic lens and adds only the weighting and apparatus that lens demands; the additions below are not new invariants.

Orthogonality

Each line captures one axis, resolved by the recurring cut of medium against the significance placed on it — the body is Biological, the beauty placed on it Cultural; materials as substance are Physical, as craft Economic, as style Cultural; script as channel is Informational, motif as form Cultural; informal manner is Social, codified taboo Judicial; self-image is Cultural, cosmology Spiritual. Where two lines would hold a fact, sharpen the seam rather than duplicate.

Aliveness

Each axis carries a standing level, a capacity-to-load ratio, and a direction of change read as a developmental phase — ascendant, mature, ossifying, decaying, or reviving — stated in the payload where it bites.

Generative Concreteness

Because the lens serves image-prompting and worldbuilding, payloads name concrete, depictable particulars — hue, form, material, silhouette, light, gesture — never bare abstraction; an unrenderable line is underspecified.

Cultural-Heavy Weighting

The Cultural domain expands into many lines as the centre of gravity; Physical, Biological, and Spiritual supply support; Economic, Political, Intellectual, Informational, and Judicial collapse toward a single line or an informative null. A thin domain is an honest signal, not an omission.

Metabolic Closure

Every domain closes on its residue — weathering, the outcast, fallen power, the marked transgressor, form-exhaustion, disenchantment — read for whether it is cleared or left to accrete; a collapsed domain may close on an explicit null ("no depictable X"), recorded as a finding, not dropped.

Evidence Discipline

Historical entities hold to the best evidence with attested myth marked as myth, not fact; fictional entities allow coherent invention extrapolated from material and aesthetic limits; nothing is fabricated as attested.

Exclusions

The audience's reaction, the entity's commercial success, its popularity, and meta-commentary are outputs of these axes, not lines of their own, and stay off the map.

M03. Header & Summary Format

Line 1 is the header: # Entity, Phase — Cultural Identity Title. The bold coordinate names the entity and its developmental phase (e.g. "Heian court, golden age" or "Weimar Berlin, late efflorescence"), since an entity's look drifts across its life and a portrait without a phase misreads it; the whole header sits in one bold span, a spaced em-dash separates the coordinate from the title, and parsers strip the leading "# " and the outer __ then split at that first em-dash, so the title contains no em-dash. Line 2 is the Summary — one cross-cutting line carrying no trailing break — naming the organising through-line (the single aesthetic thread doing the most causal work), the margin the look holds against stylistic shock, and the phase-direction of change.

M04. Output & Formatting Rules

The analysis is plain text, continuous and dense, with zero blank lines for line-by-line parsing. Line 1 is the header; Line 2 the Summary, no trailing break; section lines begin ## PXX. Title; each domain line begins with the bold key, a colon, a single space, the payload, and a single trailing

. Every domain closes on its residue line, which may be an informative null in a collapsed domain — a map missing a residue is structurally incomplete, but a residue reading "no depictable X for this entity" is complete. Completeness is by domain: all ten present and in order, each closed on its residue; the line count is not fixed and is never padded to a target. No line-number prefixes, brackets, metadata tags, or markers appear inside the block; lines are tracked by position. Payloads run roughly 20–40 words and name concrete, depictable detail. Tone is neutral and worldbuilding-oriented throughout.

M05. Exploratory Mode

Exploratory Mode is a lens-specific operating regime, not a fifth mode: it activates when the user asks to discover or generate entities rather than map a named one, and the ten-domain structure is suspended for its duration.

M06. Quick Reference

A Cultural Entity is one collective identity read for how it looks, mapped across the ten orthogonal domains weighted heavily toward Cultural, opened by a single through-line Summary.

Four modes: static portrait, discrete shift, trajectory, speculative construction.

Six invariants govern the map; the projection adds an aesthetic lens, generative concreteness, a Cultural-heavy weighting, and a phase reading of change.

Hold the boundaries: the body is Biological and its beauty Cultural; materials split Physical/Economic/Cultural by substance/craft/style; script is Informational and motif Cultural; taboo is Judicial and manner Social; cosmology is Spiritual and self-image Cultural.

Every domain closes on its residue, an informative null where the domain collapses; Economic, Political, Intellectual, Informational, and Judicial run thin by design; emergent effects (audience reaction, popularity, commercial success) are excluded.

Coordinate is entity-and-phase; the window spans a single phase, a stylistic rupture, or a drift; the through-line is named in the Summary.

Output is dense plain text, zero blank lines, payloads about 20–40 words of concrete depictable detail.

M07. Pre-Flight Checklist

Viewpoint

One collective identity read for how it looks, through an aesthetic, image-generating lens.

Orthogonal

No fact mapped twice; seams sharpened by medium-against-significance (body Biological vs beauty Cultural, substance Physical vs craft Economic vs style Cultural, script Informational vs motif Cultural, taboo Judicial vs manner Social, cosmology Spiritual vs self-image Cultural).

Alive

Every axis read as level, capacity-to-load, and a developmental-phase direction stated where it bites.

Concrete and Weighted

Payloads name depictable particulars with no unrenderable abstraction; Cultural expanded and Economic/Political/Intellectual/Informational/Judicial collapsed to a line or a null.

Significance-Ordered

Foundational to surface within each domain, residue last; the organising through-line named in the Summary, not signalled by reordering.

Metabolically Closed

Every domain's residue present and filled, or closed on an explicit informative null.

Evidence-Disciplined

Historical held to evidence with myth marked as myth; fictional coherently invented from material limits; nothing fabricated as fact.

Exclusions

Audience reaction, popularity, commercial success, and meta-commentary kept off the map.

Final Pass

Structural drift, dropped break tags, or an absent residue line corrected before rendering.

Entity, Phase — Cultural Identity Title

Summary: The entity in one breath — what it is and the phase that bounds it, the organising through-line (the single aesthetic thread doing the most causal work), the margin the look holds against stylistic shock, and the developmental direction of change.

P01. Physical Environment

Setting & Terrain: The land the entity inhabits as depictable stage — terrain, vegetation, water, horizon — the geographic frame a scene is set against.

Climate, Light & Atmosphere: The quality of light and weather as physical fact — seasonal palette, atmospheric haze, and the dominant lighting condition any image of the entity inherits.

Material Endowment: The raw substances the land yields as standing stock — stone, timber, metal, fiber, clay, pigment — the palette of matter before craft works it; substance, distinct from its craft (P03) and its style (P09).

Weathering & Ruin: The physical wear the place leaves — patina, erosion, rust, overgrowth, ruin — the visible marks of time on surface and structure.

P02. Biological Environment

Physical Type & Bearing: The look of the people — physiognomy, stature, coloring, carriage — the depictable human form characteristic of the entity, prior to dress.

Beauty Norm & Bodily Adornment: The somatic ideal and how the body itself is shaped to it — grooming, hair, tattoo, scarification, paint, deformation — distinct from worn garments in P09.

Age, Infirmity & the Image of Death: How the life-cycle's end shows — the aesthetic of elders, the infirm, and the dead; mourning, relics, memento mori, or their conspicuous absence.

P03. Economic Environment

Provision & Craft Base: The material standard and the crafts behind its objects — what is made, by whose hands, at what plenty or scarcity — read as visible backdrop, collapsed from operational detail.

Refuse & Discard: The detritus of consumption the entity throws off and how it marks the lived scene, or an informative null where the lens registers none.

P04. Social Environment

Hierarchy Made Visible: The status order as it shows on bodies and in space — who is marked high or low by dress, placement, and bearing; the visible stratification.

Bearing & Interpersonal Manner: The etiquette, gesture, proxemics, and conduct between persons — the choreography of daily social life, distinct from codified rule in P07.

The Outcast & Visible Friction: The excluded and marginal rendered as figures at the edge of the scene — the social residue the fabric throws off.

P05. Political Environment

Authority & Its Insignia: Where power sits and how it shows — regalia, banners, seats, guards, the architecture of rule — read as backdrop, collapsed from the operational detail of governance.

Fallen Power & Ruined Authority: The visible remnants of spent or toppled power — broken monuments, abandoned seats, defaced insignia — or an informative null in a stable entity.

P06. Informational Environment

Tongue, Script & Sign: The language, writing system, and notation as depictable surface — inscription, calligraphy, signage — the visible code, distinct from its expressive motifs in P09.

Illegibility & the Lost Word: The undeciphered, effaced, or forgotten script — the noise the channel leaves — or an informative null.

P07. Judicial Environment

Values, Virtues & Taboos: The celebrated virtues and strictly forbidden acts that bound conduct — the moral code as it constrains what may be worn, shown, or done; codified, distinct from informal manner in P04.

The Marked Transgressor: How violation shows — the branded, shackled, exiled, or shamed — the visible mark justice leaves on the one who breaks it, or a null.

P08. Intellectual Environment

Knowledge, Craft-Mastery & Tech Level: The developmental stage of knowing and making as it shows in the sophistication of artifact and structure — primitive, classical, advanced, or esoteric (the esoteric covering magic-as-knowledge).

Lost Knowledge & Superseded Craft: The visible remnants of an older or higher mastery now decayed — ruined technique, half-understood relics — or an informative null.

P09. Cultural Environment

Mythos, Origin & Foundational Memory: The origin story, ancestral lineage, foundational influences, and central legends that fix who the entity takes itself to be — the bedrock identity.

Core Themes & Expressive Identity: The recurring motifs and dominant ideas, and the through-line of how the culture externalizes its inner life in art, music, performance, and story.

Motif & Shape Language: The characteristic geometric forms, patterns, and design vocabulary — the entity's signature line, silhouette logic, and ornamental grammar.

Visual Rhythm & Composition: The repetition, spacing, scale, and compositional cadence governing how its visual field is arranged — dense or sparse, ordered or wild.

Color Palette: The signature hues, accents, and rules of color use — the dominant chord and the forbidden notes of its coloration.

Texture, Surface & Finish: The tactile qualities and surface treatments of its output — weave, grain, polish, patina-as-style — how its materials are made to read.

Symbolism & Encoded Meaning: The allegories and metaphorical associations embedded in its visual elements — what its emblems, colors, and figures are made to mean.

Dress, Silhouette & Ornament: The sartorial tradition — drape, silhouette, tailoring, embellishment — and the worn objects, jewelry, and arms that complete attire.

Architecture & Spatial Aesthetic: The building idiom, structural style, and spatial philosophy — how the made environment looks and how it divides public from private space.

Collective Mood & Emotional Tone: The ambient affective register the culture projects — its baseline temper and the feeling its scenes are built to evoke.

Form-Exhaustion & Staleness: The hollowing of once-potent symbols, styles, and rituals into cliché, kitsch, or anomie, and whether forms are renewed, repurposed, or left as inert residue.

P10. Spiritual Environment

Cosmology & Existential Outlook: The metaphysical model of reality and the entity's place in it — its core philosophy and posture toward fate, the sacred, and the natural world.

Ritual, Sacred Practice & Sacred Space: The ceremonies, devotional habits, and consecrated places and objects through which the sacred is enacted and made visible.

Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority: The priesthoods, doctrines, and sacred hierarchies that hold and enforce belief, and the visible form of their power.

Disenchantment & the Sacred Gone Cold: The visible residue where belief has lapsed — desecrated shrine, hollowed rite, the relic emptied of force — or an informative null in a fully enchanted entity.
