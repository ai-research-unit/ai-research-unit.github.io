# Human Framework

__Summary:__ The one framework for analysing any human subject. A study takes into account all five questions:

- WHO / WHAT — which subject is analysed, a person or a collective (E01).
- WHERE — its place and spatial reach, set by scope (the whole subject, or a named sub-area) and surroundings (analysed closed and alone, or open with what presses on it) (E02). Default: the whole subject, analysed closed.
- WHEN — the time it is analysed at, in one of four framings: a fixed point (snapshot), a turning point (event), a gradual shift (trend), or a constructed future or alternative (E03). Default: a snapshot in the subject's own present — the literal present for an ongoing subject, its own active span for a past, completed, or fictional one.
- WHY — the purpose: a plain account, or a directed one such as a fragility audit (E04). Default: a neutral, even account.
- HOW — the ten dimensions it is analysed through (E05).

The shared format every study is written in — the header, the Summary line, the dimension sections — is fixed in E06.

## E01. WHO / WHAT — The Subject
■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■

The subject is either one human person or one human collective in the broad sense — an organisation, institution, society, city, culture, movement, or market — in a given condition, real or fictional. All that HOW requires is one bounded thing, with an inside and an outside. Where the input names not a subject but an event, relationship, or period, the subject it ranges over is induced from it, and the study runs on one such subject — the default, set in E02 — or, where the parties act as a single unit (an alliance, a partnership, a merged body), on the one composite collective they form, never on the event itself.

The subject is induced from the input, or set to its defaults where the input is silent.

## E02. WHERE — Place
■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■

WHERE pins the study's place and spatial reach, set by scope and surroundings. Scope — the whole subject, or one named sub-area of it: the capital within a state, one site within an organisation, one quarter within a city. Surroundings — the subject analysed closed (alone) or open (with the environment, neighbours, and counterparties that press on it); an open study still covers only the subject's own side, describing its interfaces with neighbours but never a neighbour as a subject in its own right. Left unset, both rest at base: the whole subject, closed. Scale sits in WHERE too, while the dimensions stay scale-independent (E05). Scope, surroundings, and scale are induced from the input where it implies them: a relational input — a war, a rivalry, a market against its competitors — defaults to one side as the subject, that side open against the other, rather than the two as one composite — which E01 reserves for parties that act as a single unit.

WHERE is central for a place or a society and thin for a life — little more than where the life is set; a study leaves it at default unless it carries real weight. The WHERE, where it is set, joins the WHEN in the Setting.

The WHERE is induced from the input, or set to its defaults where the input is silent.

## E03. WHEN — Time
■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■

WHEN pins the study to a time. All years are written with CE or BCE. It takes one of four framings; left unset, it defaults to the first of them — a Static Snapshot of the subject in its own active present: the literal present for an ongoing subject, the subject's own active span for a past, completed, or fictional one.

### Static Snapshot

The subject as it stands at a fixed point.

### Discrete Event

A rapid, disruptive turning point, bracketed from rupture to settling; treated as a natural experiment, an event grades what was merely claimed against what actually held — the one framing whose outcome can be treated as a result rather than a claim.

### Long-Term Trend

A gradual shift with no single flashpoint, long on the subject's own timescale — multi-year to multi-generational — bracketed across the transition.

### Speculative Construction

A coherent extrapolation or invention from established limits — a future or an alternative projected forward from the subject's real limits.

The WHEN is induced from the input, or set to its defaults where the input is silent.

## E04. WHY — Purpose
■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■

WHY pins what the subject is analysed for — which study the output is. The answer is one of a short, fixed set of purposes, the default being the neutral study. Each purpose directs every dimension at one thing and carries its own WHEN default (still overridable); it changes what each dimension's description reports, not the format — the output stays the ten descriptions and the Summary of E06. WHY can be induced from the input or named explicitly, defaulting to neutral when neither applies.

- Neutral — every dimension analysed evenly, privileging nothing. The default; WHEN as induced (E03).
- Formation-genealogy — every dimension analysed for what produced the subject's present state. WHEN defaults to a Long-Term Trend, past → now.
- Fragility-audit — every dimension analysed for what could break the subject, the chief fault named in the Summary. WHEN defaults forward.
- Potential-audit — every dimension analysed for what could lift the subject, the chief opening named in the Summary. WHEN defaults forward.
- Strategic-outlook — every dimension analysed for the subject's trajectory and its standing against others, and what that implies for an outside party. WHEN defaults forward, WHERE often open.

Bare forecast is not a purpose: the undirected forward projection is the neutral study at a forward WHEN (E03), since it selects no direction. A purpose must fit its single line — no sub-divisions, scoring, or excluded matters of its own; the five above all fit.

The WHY is induced from the input, or set to its defaults where the input is silent. Where WHEN or WHERE is fixed by more than one of these at once, precedence runs highest-first: an explicit instruction, then what the input induces, then the purpose's own default, then the base default of E02–E03.

## E05. HOW — The Ten Dimensions
■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■

The study analyses the subject through ten dimensions, D01–D10, each closing on a residue: D01 Physical, D02 Biological, D03 Economic, D04 Social, D05 Emotional, D06 Informational, D07 Moral, D08 Intellectual, D09 Cultural, D10 Spiritual. The ten are treated as exhaustive and exclusive: together they aim to leave nothing out, and every fact is assigned to exactly one. The unit assigned is the fact, not the object: one object — a temple, a coin — splits into several facts across several dimensions (the split rule below). This is a discipline enforced by the boundary rule below, not a demonstrated property — anything that resists placement is routed by adjudication, never dropped. They sort questions, not the world: the dimensions are coupled in reality — terrain shapes organisation, debt feeds grievance — but kept apart by the question each asks. Each is defined at the bottom of this document, which doubles as the blank template a study fills.

### Boundaries

The dimensions meet at boundaries — the lines where one ends and the next begins. Placement runs by ownership first, then a routine split, then a lookup. A dimension's ownership is its scope as set in its template entry below: the dimension whose scope covers a thing takes it. Ownership settles the clear cases; the split and the lookup table carry the rest — and most real cases reach the table. Where one object carries both a thing and the significance placed on it, the two are split and each assigned to its owner — the routine case: the material, apparatus, channel, or body sits in the dimension that owns the thing; the permission, status, rule, meaning, method, affect, will, or cosmology attached to it sits in the dimension that owns the significance. Where ownership and the split still leave a fact on either side, the pairwise table below carries the adjudication. So the ten stay exhaustive and exclusive by adjudication, not by nature. The boundaries, pair by pair:

- Physical / Economic — the endowment of substances, energy, and terrain is Physical; its conversion into work, goods, provisioning, and cost is Economic; the discharge conversion throws off is Economic, while the saturation of the sink that absorbs it is Physical.
- Biological / Economic / Moral — bodies, health, population, and the capacity to heal them are Biological; the food, medicine, and goods supply that sustains them is Economic, as is the fiscal cost of the dependent load they leave; the permission to use a treatment or substance is Moral.
- Biological / Emotional — the nervous system and the body as organ are Biological; the emotion, mood, will, and felt drive they produce are Emotional — the same organ-versus-output split the framework draws for the brain, where the organ is Biological and the knowing it carries is Intellectual.
- Biological / Intellectual — the brain and nervous system as organ are Biological; the knowing, method, and skill they carry are Intellectual.
- Biological / Cultural-Social-Moral-Spiritual — the body, living or dead, is Biological; the meaning placed on it — beauty, status, taboo, or the rite that enacts the sacred around it — is Cultural, Social, Moral, or Spiritual.
- Physical / Cultural-Social-Moral-Spiritual — the made thing, building, or monument is Physical; the meaning placed on it — landmark and character Cultural, status Social, taboo Moral, the sanctity of a relic or sacred site Spiritual — sits with whichever owns the significance, the same object-versus-significance split the body line above draws.
- Economic / Social / Emotional — the holding and flow of goods, and the surplus including tax, tribute, and rent collected and reallocated, are Economic; the status meaning of holdings, and the apparatus that levies and collects, are Social; the political will to tax and the alignments behind cross-border flows are Emotional.
- Economic / Moral — the material flow is Economic; who is permitted to do what with it is Moral.
- Economic / Informational — the grid, plant, and utility that power a channel are Economic; the channel's bandwidth, uptime, reach, and openness are Informational — power and channel dissociate.
- Economic / Cultural — the provisioning flow and the working of matter into goods are Economic; the style of the working and the experience made of the flow are Cultural.
- Social / Moral — informal norm and custom are Social, as is the apparatus that administers and enforces conduct; the codified rule itself, its adjudication, and the warrant to sanction are Moral.
- Social / Emotional — the structure and apparatus — networks, institutions, bureaucracy, the offices of authority, and the organised means of force — are Social; the will, leadership, sentiment, trust, and temper that direct or animate them are Emotional. The disorder and decay the apparatus fails to hold are the Social residue; the distrust and failure of will that affect leaves are the Emotional residue.
- Social / Cultural / Emotional — the structural group and its material grievance are Social; the symbolic story of identity and the meaning placed on the group are Cultural; the radius of interpersonal trust and the collective temper of the public are Emotional; as residues, unprocessed grievance is Social while anomie and meaning-erosion are Cultural.
- Emotional / Moral / Informational — the binding command and the will behind it are Emotional, its rule and warrant are Moral; the framing and carrying of the message are Informational. The integrity of the record and the figures a subject reports about itself is Informational, whatever they describe.
- Cultural / Emotional — the story of peoplehood, identity, and the justifying narrative of authority are Cultural; the felt allegiance, fervour, or disaffection toward them is Emotional.
- Informational / Intellectual — the carrying of meaning is Informational; the truth-seeking and method behind it are Intellectual.
- Informational / Cultural — the channel and medium are Informational; the expressive form is Cultural.
- Informational / Social / Cultural — the record, file, trail, and circulating reputation as content are Informational; the standing they confer is Social; the identity they project is Cultural.
- Intellectual / Economic — knowing and method are Intellectual; their embodiment in tools and production is Economic.
- Intellectual / Spiritual — the empirical paradigm is Intellectual; the metaphysical frame is Spiritual.
- Cultural / Spiritual — the expression and self-image are Cultural; the cosmology behind them is Spiritual.
- Spiritual / Social / Emotional — belief, the holy, the rite that enacts it, and the religious institution as holder of doctrine, property, and reach are Spiritual; its organisation, apparatus, and any force it wields are Social; the leadership, devotion, and fervour it commands are Emotional.

Where a fact still seems to fit two dimensions, apply the order above — ownership, then the split; sharpen the boundary rather than duplicate the fact. Each dimension's residue line carries only its own discharge, and cross-feeds between residues are named in prose, never duplicated as lines — disaffection in Emotional, for instance, feeds the disorder in Social. Compositions of these boundaries recur and are registered here without becoming new ones — the keyperson split, for instance, places the holder's body in Biological, the scarce skill in Intellectual, the office in Social, and the leadership in Emotional. This table is the master: a new boundary, once any study needs one, is added here in generalised form, and any recurring composition is registered above it, so the boundaries stay in one place. A module states none of its own — it carries no boundaries, by E07.

### Closure

Every dimension's description closes by naming the residue it discharges, analysed for whether it is cleared or left to accrete. A thin dimension may close on a meaningful blank, but the residue is never omitted.

### Ordering

Within each dimension and across the ten, order along the framework's own gradient — roughly from material substrate to metaphysical frame, with an apparatus placed before the will that animates it, as Social precedes Emotional — the residue last; the single most causal factor is named in the Summary, never signalled by reordering.

### Scale and Timescale Independence

The same ten dimensions fit a band or an empire, a place or a culture, an afternoon or ten millennia; the set is never extended or pruned, only answered differently. Scale answers to WHERE (E02) and timescale to WHEN (E03); the dimensions depend on neither.

### Outcomes, Not Dimensions

A complex characterisation — an individual's personality, a collective's character or regime type — is not a dimension but a pattern across several. It is never given its own dimension; where it is the most causal factor it is named in the Summary, otherwise left to emerge from the dimensions that compose it.

## E06. Output
■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■

Adapt the style of the study to the subject: if it is academic, you can use an academic style. But if the subject is from everyday life, for example assessing one city nowadays, use plain, everyday words, not academic style. 

Match the style to the subject and the reader, not to the source material. Everyday subjects get plain, everyday words, even if the material I'm working from is academic. Use an academic style only when the subject is technical and I've asked for it.

NO OVER-ENGINEERING, NO over-confidence, NO verbiage, NO jargon, NO metaphors.

Each line of the study is separated from the next by a single blank line, and nothing outside it — the study is the entire output. The study contains only what the format defines — header, Summary, and the ten dimension sections — with no prefatory notes, scope disclaimers, methodological commentary, or other insertions between or around them.

Line 1 is the header — a top-level heading holding one bold span, which carries the Setting (the WHEN, and the WHERE where it is set) and the Subject Title (the WHO/WHAT), separated by a spaced em-dash. The Setting sits left, the title right, and the title carries no em-dash. (Parsing note: a span inside the Setting uses a spaced en-dash, Start – End, not an em-dash, and any commas a multi-part Setting carries are likewise preserved, so a split on the first em-dash cleanly divides Setting from title.)

Line 2 is: Summary — a summary of the study, highlighting the single element doing the most causal work

Section lines begin ## DXX. Title. In the neutral study each dimension holds a single unlabelled description, its residue last, separated from the next by a blank line; under a loaded module each dimension instead holds that module's labelled aspect-lines (E07), residue last, in the same blank-line-separated layout.

No line-number prefixes, square-bracket metadata tags, or dimension cross-reference codes appear in a description. Where a description must mark a boundary, it names the neighbouring dimension in prose, never its DXX code, which appears only at the head of its own section line. A filled study line runs roughly 20–40 words, a benchmark for compression, not a hard limit; the generic dimension definitions in the template below run longer, since each enumerates a whole dimension's scope, and the Summary of line 2 is exempt, running as long as it needs. Do not insert manual line breaks within a paragraph or description; let prose run to its natural length and break only at sentence ends or explicit paragraph breaks.

Completeness is by dimension: all ten present, in order, each closed on its residue. The bottom of this document gives the template in exactly this form — one unlabelled description per dimension, closing on its residue. Filled in for an actual subject, it is the neutral study. A named purpose keeps this format and changes only what each description reports (E04); a loaded specialisation module instead replaces each dimension's single description with that module's labelled aspect-lines, the form set out in E07 — the header and Summary unchanged.

Refused temptations — neither talked up nor talked down: no flattering distortion one way and no alarmist distortion the other beyond what the evidence carries, with the same instruments applied whether the subject is liked or disliked, near or far, self or other.

## E07. Specialisation Modules
■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■■

A specialisation module specialises the study to a kind of subject solely through the aspects it gives each dimension — the sub-parts that kind divides the dimension into. It does nothing else: WHO, WHERE, WHEN, and WHY are fixed only as E01–E04 provide, never by a module. The neutral study writes each dimension as one description; a loaded module replaces that single description with the module's labelled aspect-lines — a bold label, a colon, a space, then the text, residue last — otherwise in the format of E06. Whenever a module is present in context, every dimension takes this labelled form; the study never falls back to the unlabelled paragraph. The labels, and how many each dimension carries, are the module's own and are used as it sets them — neither a fixed count nor improvised.

A module is a separate document, used alongside this one, never on its own. It supplies only the dimension aspects specific to its kind, and never repeats what this document already fixes — the subject, the ten dimensions, the rules, the boundaries, the format. Every boundary an aspect-line touches lives in the master table of E05, named there in prose; a module aspect never restates which dimension a neighbouring piece belongs to. It may still name a residue cross-feed in prose, as E05 allows, since that is dynamics, not placement.

# __Setting — Subject Title__

__Summary:__ The subject in brief — what it is, the Setting bounding it, and the single most causal factor (binding constraint, central tension, organising through-line, or hinge, by the study).

## D01. Physical Dimension

The inert material stage — terrain and location, climate and atmosphere, hazard exposure, and the standing endowment of substances and energy the subject can draw on. It discharges a physical residue: the saturated sink — the load the surroundings have absorbed and not cleared — and the wear it leaves: settled pollution, weathering, ruin.

## D02. Biological Dimension

The living substrate — the bodies and their type, the disease and mortality baseline, vitality and the capacity to heal, and reproduction and density against the carrying ceiling. It discharges the residue of decline and dependency: the standing load of age, infirmity, and death the life-cycle leaves, and the healing capacity that meets it.

## D03. Economic Dimension

The conversion of endowment into life and surplus — the provisioning floor, the energy and means of production, exchange and logistics, the value-medium that stores and defers worth, and the concentration and spread of wealth. It discharges waste and imbalance: on the material side the outflow the conversion throws off — refuse, effluent, the detritus of consumption as it leaves the process; on the financial side the accumulated mispricing surplus leaves behind — malinvestment, bad debt, price-drift.

## D04. Social Dimension

The structure of human association, informal and formal — the cohesion of the structure, the stratification of class and status, the norms, customs, and bearing that govern conduct, and the families, guilds, and factions below the whole; and, at scale, the whole apparatus a body acts through — its organisations, institutions, and bureaucracy, the offices of authority, and the organised means of force — with its administrative capacity and its standing relations with other bodies beyond its bounds. It discharges friction and dysfunction: the grievance the fabric throws off, the excluded it leaves, the disorder the apparatus fails to contain, and the sclerosis or collapse of the apparatus when it ossifies or fails.

## D05. Emotional Dimension

The will and affect — the felt drive that moves the subject to act and the sentiment that colours it. In a person it is emotion, mood, and individual will: fear, anger, desire, grief, trust or suspicion, the resolve to act, the temper of the inner life. In a collective it is the political will and the leadership that direct it, and the public sentiment that backs or resists them — élan, fervour, panic, allegiance or disaffection, and the radius of trust across the public. It discharges distrust, apathy, and despair: the estrangement, the failure of will, and the exhaustion the drive leaves when spent or thwarted.

## D06. Informational Dimension

The movement of meaning — the shared code that makes minds mutually legible, the channels and their reach, the media that fix and store messages across time, and the framing, control, and surveillance of what may be said. It discharges noise: the signal-decay, distortion, and corruption the channel throws off.

## D07. Moral Dimension

The binding ought — the standard of right and wrong, the foundations and values beneath any code, the codified law and the taboos that mark the forbidden, and the adjudication, the rights it confers, and the sanction it warrants. It discharges impunity and transgression: the gap between justice owed and delivered, and the marks left on the transgressor.

## D08. Intellectual Dimension

The understanding of reality — the inquiry and paradigm that frame what counts as knowing, the applied method and craft that turn it into technique, and the stock, schooling, and distribution of skill. It discharges obsolescence and loss: the superseded theory shed and the knowledge decayed or broken in transmission.

## D09. Cultural Dimension

The expressive surface — the mythos, memory, and identity that fix who the subject takes itself to be, and the artistic and material expression through which it externalises that. It discharges form-exhaustion: the hollowing of once-potent symbols and styles into cliché, anomie, and staleness.

## D10. Spiritual Dimension

The metaphysical frame — the cosmology and existential outlook that place the subject in reality, the ritual and sacred through which that is enacted, and the religious institutions and doctrinal authority that hold it. It discharges disenchantment: the residue where a cosmology loses its grip — secularising drift, the void, the sacred gone cold.
