# __Cultural Entity Framework__
__Summary:__ The framework for reading any collective identity — real or fictional — for how it looks, mapped across ten orthogonal domains weighted heavily toward the Cultural domain through an aesthetic, image-generating lens.

# __Explanations__
## __M01. What a Cultural Entity Is__
A Cultural Entity is any collective identity — real or fictional — read for how it looks, mapped across ten orthogonal domains but weighted heavily toward the Cultural domain through an aesthetic, image-generating lens. The unit is one collective identity (a culture, subculture, civilization, mythic society, fictional realm, aesthetic or ideological movement, or a species with cultural traits); the question put to every domain is how it looks and feels rendered, never how it operates from within. A single through-line Summary opens the map. The four modes share the domain set and differ only in window and change-reading.<br><br>
### Static Portrait
The entity's standing aesthetic at a fixed phase.<br><br>
### Discrete Shift
A rapid stylistic rupture — conquest, conversion, revolution, contact — bracketed from break to settling.<br><br>
### Trajectory
A multi-generational stylistic drift with no single flashpoint, bracketed across the arc.<br><br>
### Speculative Construction
A coherent invented identity extrapolated from established material and aesthetic limits.<br><br>

## __M02. Core Design Principles__
Six invariants govern the map — orthogonality, aliveness, metabolic closure, significance ordering, scale invariance, evidence discipline. This projection fixes them to an aesthetic lens and adds only the weighting and apparatus that lens demands; the additions below are not new invariants.<br><br>
### Orthogonality
Each line captures one axis, resolved by the recurring cut of medium against the significance placed on it — the body is Biological, the beauty placed on it Cultural; materials as substance are Physical, as craft Economic, as style Cultural; script as channel is Informational, motif as form Cultural; informal manner is Social, codified taboo Judicial; self-image is Cultural, cosmology Spiritual. Where two lines would hold a fact, sharpen the seam rather than duplicate.<br><br>
### Aliveness
Each axis carries a standing level, a capacity-to-load ratio, and a direction of change read as a developmental phase — ascendant, mature, ossifying, decaying, or reviving — stated in the payload where it bites.<br><br>
### Generative Concreteness
Because the lens serves image-prompting and worldbuilding, payloads name concrete, depictable particulars — hue, form, material, silhouette, light, gesture — never bare abstraction; an unrenderable line is underspecified.<br><br>
### Cultural-Heavy Weighting
The Cultural domain expands into many lines as the centre of gravity; Physical, Biological, and Spiritual supply support; Economic, Political, Intellectual, Informational, and Judicial collapse toward a single line or an informative null. A thin domain is an honest signal, not an omission.<br><br>
### Metabolic Closure
Every domain closes on its residue — weathering, the outcast, fallen power, the marked transgressor, form-exhaustion, disenchantment — read for whether it is cleared or left to accrete; a collapsed domain may close on an explicit null ("no depictable X"), recorded as a finding, not dropped.<br><br>
### Evidence Discipline
Historical entities hold to the best evidence with attested myth marked as myth, not fact; fictional entities allow coherent invention extrapolated from material and aesthetic limits; nothing is fabricated as attested.<br><br>
### Exclusions
The audience's reaction, the entity's commercial success, its popularity, and meta-commentary are outputs of these axes, not lines of their own, and stay off the map.<br><br>

## __M03. Header & Summary Format__
Line 1 is the header: # __Entity, Phase — Cultural Identity Title__. The bold coordinate names the entity and its developmental phase (e.g. "Heian court, golden age" or "Weimar Berlin, late efflorescence"), since an entity's look drifts across its life and a portrait without a phase misreads it; the whole header sits in one bold span, a spaced em-dash separates the coordinate from the title, and parsers strip the leading "# " and the outer __ then split at that first em-dash, so the title contains no em-dash. Line 2 is the Summary — one cross-cutting line carrying no trailing break — naming the organising through-line (the single aesthetic thread doing the most causal work), the margin the look holds against stylistic shock, and the phase-direction of change.<br><br>

## __M04. Output & Formatting Rules__
The analysis is plain text, continuous and dense, with zero blank lines for line-by-line parsing. Line 1 is the header; Line 2 the Summary, no trailing break; section lines begin ## PXX. Title; each domain line begins with the bold key, a colon, a single space, the payload, and a single trailing <br><br>. Every domain closes on its residue line, which may be an informative null in a collapsed domain — a map missing a residue is structurally incomplete, but a residue reading "no depictable X for this entity" is complete. Completeness is by domain: all ten present and in order, each closed on its residue; the line count is not fixed and is never padded to a target. No line-number prefixes, brackets, metadata tags, or markers appear inside the block; lines are tracked by position. Payloads run roughly 20–40 words and name concrete, depictable detail. Tone is neutral and worldbuilding-oriented throughout.<br><br>

## __M05. Exploratory Mode__
Exploratory Mode is a lens-specific operating regime, not a fifth mode: it activates when the user asks to discover or generate entities rather than map a named one, and the ten-domain structure is suspended for its duration.<br><br>

## __M06. Quick Reference__
A Cultural Entity is one collective identity read for how it looks, mapped across the ten orthogonal domains weighted heavily toward Cultural, opened by a single through-line Summary.<br><br>
Four modes: static portrait, discrete shift, trajectory, speculative construction.<br><br>
Six invariants govern the map; the projection adds an aesthetic lens, generative concreteness, a Cultural-heavy weighting, and a phase reading of change.<br><br>
Hold the boundaries: the body is Biological and its beauty Cultural; materials split Physical/Economic/Cultural by substance/craft/style; script is Informational and motif Cultural; taboo is Judicial and manner Social; cosmology is Spiritual and self-image Cultural.<br><br>
Every domain closes on its residue, an informative null where the domain collapses; Economic, Political, Intellectual, Informational, and Judicial run thin by design; emergent effects (audience reaction, popularity, commercial success) are excluded.<br><br>
Coordinate is entity-and-phase; the window spans a single phase, a stylistic rupture, or a drift; the through-line is named in the Summary.<br><br>
Output is dense plain text, zero blank lines, payloads about 20–40 words of concrete depictable detail.<br><br>

## __M07. Pre-Flight Checklist__
### Viewpoint
One collective identity read for how it looks, through an aesthetic, image-generating lens.<br><br>
### Orthogonal
No fact mapped twice; seams sharpened by medium-against-significance (body Biological vs beauty Cultural, substance Physical vs craft Economic vs style Cultural, script Informational vs motif Cultural, taboo Judicial vs manner Social, cosmology Spiritual vs self-image Cultural).<br><br>
### Alive
Every axis read as level, capacity-to-load, and a developmental-phase direction stated where it bites.<br><br>
### Concrete and Weighted
Payloads name depictable particulars with no unrenderable abstraction; Cultural expanded and Economic/Political/Intellectual/Informational/Judicial collapsed to a line or a null.<br><br>
### Significance-Ordered
Foundational to surface within each domain, residue last; the organising through-line named in the Summary, not signalled by reordering.<br><br>
### Metabolically Closed
Every domain's residue present and filled, or closed on an explicit informative null.<br><br>
### Evidence-Disciplined
Historical held to evidence with myth marked as myth; fictional coherently invented from material limits; nothing fabricated as fact.<br><br>
### Exclusions
Audience reaction, popularity, commercial success, and meta-commentary kept off the map.<br><br>
### Final Pass
Structural drift, dropped break tags, or an absent residue line corrected before rendering.<br><br>



# __Entity, Phase — Cultural Identity Title__
__Summary:__ The entity in one breath — what it is and the phase that bounds it, the organising through-line (the single aesthetic thread doing the most causal work), the margin the look holds against stylistic shock, and the developmental direction of change.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Setting & Terrain:__ The land the entity inhabits as depictable stage — terrain, vegetation, water, horizon — the geographic frame a scene is set against.<br><br>
__Climate, Light & Atmosphere:__ The quality of light and weather as physical fact — seasonal palette, atmospheric haze, and the dominant lighting condition any image of the entity inherits.<br><br>
__Material Endowment:__ The raw substances the land yields as standing stock — stone, timber, metal, fiber, clay, pigment — the palette of matter before craft works it; substance, distinct from its craft (P03) and its style (P09).<br><br>
__Weathering & Ruin:__ The physical wear the place leaves — patina, erosion, rust, overgrowth, ruin — the visible marks of time on surface and structure.<br><br>
## __P02. Biological Environment__
__Physical Type & Bearing:__ The look of the people — physiognomy, stature, coloring, carriage — the depictable human form characteristic of the entity, prior to dress.<br><br>
__Beauty Norm & Bodily Adornment:__ The somatic ideal and how the body itself is shaped to it — grooming, hair, tattoo, scarification, paint, deformation — distinct from worn garments in P09.<br><br>
__Age, Infirmity & the Image of Death:__ How the life-cycle's end shows — the aesthetic of elders, the infirm, and the dead; mourning, relics, memento mori, or their conspicuous absence.<br><br>
## __P03. Economic Environment__
__Provision & Craft Base:__ The material standard and the crafts behind its objects — what is made, by whose hands, at what plenty or scarcity — read as visible backdrop, collapsed from operational detail.<br><br>
__Refuse & Discard:__ The detritus of consumption the entity throws off and how it marks the lived scene, or an informative null where the lens registers none.<br><br>
## __P04. Social Environment__
__Hierarchy Made Visible:__ The status order as it shows on bodies and in space — who is marked high or low by dress, placement, and bearing; the visible stratification.<br><br>
__Bearing & Interpersonal Manner:__ The etiquette, gesture, proxemics, and conduct between persons — the choreography of daily social life, distinct from codified rule in P07.<br><br>
__The Outcast & Visible Friction:__ The excluded and marginal rendered as figures at the edge of the scene — the social residue the fabric throws off.<br><br>
## __P05. Political Environment__
__Authority & Its Insignia:__ Where power sits and how it shows — regalia, banners, seats, guards, the architecture of rule — read as backdrop, collapsed from the operational detail of governance.<br><br>
__Fallen Power & Ruined Authority:__ The visible remnants of spent or toppled power — broken monuments, abandoned seats, defaced insignia — or an informative null in a stable entity.<br><br>
## __P06. Informational Environment__
__Tongue, Script & Sign:__ The language, writing system, and notation as depictable surface — inscription, calligraphy, signage — the visible code, distinct from its expressive motifs in P09.<br><br>
__Illegibility & the Lost Word:__ The undeciphered, effaced, or forgotten script — the noise the channel leaves — or an informative null.<br><br>
## __P07. Judicial Environment__
__Values, Virtues & Taboos:__ The celebrated virtues and strictly forbidden acts that bound conduct — the moral code as it constrains what may be worn, shown, or done; codified, distinct from informal manner in P04.<br><br>
__The Marked Transgressor:__ How violation shows — the branded, shackled, exiled, or shamed — the visible mark justice leaves on the one who breaks it, or a null.<br><br>
## __P08. Intellectual Environment__
__Knowledge, Craft-Mastery & Tech Level:__ The developmental stage of knowing and making as it shows in the sophistication of artifact and structure — primitive, classical, advanced, or esoteric (the esoteric covering magic-as-knowledge).<br><br>
__Lost Knowledge & Superseded Craft:__ The visible remnants of an older or higher mastery now decayed — ruined technique, half-understood relics — or an informative null.<br><br>
## __P09. Cultural Environment__
__Mythos, Origin & Foundational Memory:__ The origin story, ancestral lineage, foundational influences, and central legends that fix who the entity takes itself to be — the bedrock identity.<br><br>
__Core Themes & Expressive Identity:__ The recurring motifs and dominant ideas, and the through-line of how the culture externalizes its inner life in art, music, performance, and story.<br><br>
__Motif & Shape Language:__ The characteristic geometric forms, patterns, and design vocabulary — the entity's signature line, silhouette logic, and ornamental grammar.<br><br>
__Visual Rhythm & Composition:__ The repetition, spacing, scale, and compositional cadence governing how its visual field is arranged — dense or sparse, ordered or wild.<br><br>
__Color Palette:__ The signature hues, accents, and rules of color use — the dominant chord and the forbidden notes of its coloration.<br><br>
__Texture, Surface & Finish:__ The tactile qualities and surface treatments of its output — weave, grain, polish, patina-as-style — how its materials are made to read.<br><br>
__Symbolism & Encoded Meaning:__ The allegories and metaphorical associations embedded in its visual elements — what its emblems, colors, and figures are made to mean.<br><br>
__Dress, Silhouette & Ornament:__ The sartorial tradition — drape, silhouette, tailoring, embellishment — and the worn objects, jewelry, and arms that complete attire.<br><br>
__Architecture & Spatial Aesthetic:__ The building idiom, structural style, and spatial philosophy — how the made environment looks and how it divides public from private space.<br><br>
__Collective Mood & Emotional Tone:__ The ambient affective register the culture projects — its baseline temper and the feeling its scenes are built to evoke.<br><br>
__Form-Exhaustion & Staleness:__ The hollowing of once-potent symbols, styles, and rituals into cliché, kitsch, or anomie, and whether forms are renewed, repurposed, or left as inert residue.<br><br>
## __P10. Spiritual Environment__
__Cosmology & Existential Outlook:__ The metaphysical model of reality and the entity's place in it — its core philosophy and posture toward fate, the sacred, and the natural world.<br><br>
__Ritual, Sacred Practice & Sacred Space:__ The ceremonies, devotional habits, and consecrated places and objects through which the sacred is enacted and made visible.<br><br>
__Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority:__ The priesthoods, doctrines, and sacred hierarchies that hold and enforce belief, and the visible form of their power.<br><br>
__Disenchantment & the Sacred Gone Cold:__ The visible residue where belief has lapsed — desecrated shrine, hollowed rite, the relic emptied of force — or an informative null in a fully enchanted entity.<br><br>
